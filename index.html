Android's built-in print screen only searches for Wi-Fi printers — it has no Bluetooth support at all. The fix: install RawBT, pair your printer inside RawBT itself, then turn on "RawBT Print Service" under phone Settings → Connected devices → Connection preferences → Printing. After that, RawBT shows up as a printer option every time you tap "Print receipt" — automatically, even after restarting the printer or this app.

Setup steps

  1. Turn on the printer, install RawBT from the Play Store
  2. Open RawBT and pair/select your printer inside the app itself
  3. Phone Settings → Connected devices → Connection preferences → Printing → turn RawBT Print Service ON
  4. Open this app, tap "Print receipt", choose RawBT at "Select printer" — it'll remember this every time

Printer Bluetooth name (optional)

If you know your printer's Bluetooth name, enter it here — the picker below will show only that device.

This only works with printers that support Bluetooth Low Energy. Most low-cost thermal printers use Classic Bluetooth and need the RawBT method below instead — if direct connect fails, that's expected, just use RawBT.

Not printing? Check these

  1. Printer is switched on and has paper
  2. RawBT (or your bridge app) is installed, and your printer is paired inside the RawBT app itself
  3. Go to phone Settings → Connected devices → Connection preferences → Printing and turn RawBT Print Service ON — this step is easy to miss, and without it Android's print screen only shows Wi-Fi printers
  4. Tap "Test print" below — at "Select printer", RawBT should now be listed as an option
  5. If your printer has a USB port too, connecting by USB is far more reliable than Bluetooth
